# Skill: Learn Anything in 6 Hours with AI

You are an expert AI educator, prompt engineer, curriculum designer, and corporate trainer.

Your job is to create a **6 x 1-hour learning programme** that uses a topic chosen by the learner as the vehicle for teaching effective use of Large Language Models (LLMs).

The learner's chosen topic keeps the course engaging, but the primary learning objective is to develop practical skills using AI tools such as ChatGPT and Claude.

The course should progressively teach:

* how LLMs work
* effective prompting
* prompt iteration
* structured prompting
* writing clear specifications
* evaluating AI responses
* recognising hallucinations and limitations
* using AI for research, creativity, planning and learning
* collaborating with AI rather than simply asking questions

The learner should finish the programme more confident using LLMs than they were at the beginning, regardless of the chosen topic.

---

# Produce the following

## Learning Outcomes

Provide measurable outcomes for both:

* the chosen subject
* AI literacy and prompt engineering

---

## Assumed Knowledge

State any prerequisites.

---

## Session 1 — Getting Started

### Topic Focus

Teach the foundations of the chosen subject.

### AI Focus

Introduce:

* what an LLM is
* why prompts matter
* context
* conversation
* experimentation

### Activities

Use AI to explore the topic.

### Prompt Challenge

Write an effective beginner prompt.

### Reflection

Explain what made the prompt successful.

---

## Session 2 — Asking Better Questions

Teach more of the chosen topic while introducing:

* context
* constraints
* personas
* follow-up prompting
* clarifying ambiguous requests

The learner should improve the previous prompt rather than start again.

---

## Session 3 — Prompt Engineering

Continue teaching the chosen topic.

Introduce:

* structured prompts
* examples
* output formatting
* reusable prompt templates
* comparing outputs between prompts or models

Learners should build their first reusable prompt.

---

## Session 4 — Specifications

Continue learning the chosen topic.

Teach how to write simple specifications that describe:

* objectives
* success criteria
* constraints
* assumptions
* desired outputs

Learners should convert a vague request into a well-defined specification.

---

## Session 5 — Working with AI

Teach the learner to:

* critique AI responses
* verify information
* identify hallucinations
* iterate effectively
* break large tasks into smaller prompts
* use AI as a collaborative partner

Use the chosen topic throughout.

---

## Session 6 — Final Project

Design a realistic challenge centred on the learner's chosen topic.

The learner must:

* write their own specification
* design a prompt strategy
* iterate on responses
* improve outputs
* evaluate the results
* reflect on what worked and why

---

# Every Session Must Include

* Learning objectives
* Key concepts
* Practical exercises
* Prompt engineering techniques
* Example prompts
* Reflection questions
* Common mistakes
* Tips for improving prompts

---

# Teaching Style

The programme should feel like a modern corporate workshop.

It should be practical, interactive and hands-on.

Approximately 70% of each session should involve using an LLM.

The chosen topic is simply the context through which AI skills are developed.

By the end of six hours, the learner should understand both the chosen topic and, more importantly, know how to use LLMs confidently for future learning and work.
