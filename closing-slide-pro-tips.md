# Slide: Top 5 Things I Wish I Knew Earlier

## 💡 The Pro-Level Mindset Shifts for Elite AI Fluency

### 1. The "Vibe Check" Toggle (Fast vs. Deep)
*   **The Trap:** Allowing the AI to drown you in exhaustive, polite paragraphs when you are just spitballing ideas.
*   **The Pro Move:** Explicitly dictate the interaction speed. Tell the model: 
    > *"Give me raw bullet points and ultra-short summaries while we are vibing/ideating. I will tell you when I am ready for the full-length output."*

### 2. Global Security & Guardrails are Non-Negotiable
*   **The Trap:** Treating every new chat window like an isolated sandbox and forgetting data boundaries.
*   **The Pro Move:** Enterprise-grade security must be baked into your workflow. Always anchor your long-term workspace skills with permanent data privacy, compliance constraints, and strict execution boundaries so the model never drifts or leaks.

### 3. Multi-Pass Construction (Don't Do It All at Once)
*   **The Trap:** Asking an LLM to research, outline, write, format, and debug a massive project all in a single, massive prompt. This dilutes the quality of every step.
*   **The Pro Move:** Break tasks into separate modular passes. Execute Pass 1 for structural ideation, Pass 2 for content drafting, and Pass 3 for strict formatting and edge-case checking.

### 4. Actively Design Against the "Happy Path"
*   **The Trap:** Assuming everything will go perfectly. Generic prompts yield lazy, flawless-scenario answers that fall apart under real-world friction.
*   **The Pro Move:** Explicitly force the AI to handle failure. Inject negative constraints into your frameworks: 
    > *"If data point X or constraint Y is missing, do not guess or hallucinate—stop immediately and output a clear, structured error flag."*

### 5. Stop Typing, Start Architecting
*   **The Trap:** Falling into the lazy habit of treating the prompt box like a conversational Google search bar.
*   **The Pro Move:** Treat the chat window like a software canvas. Stop asking casual questions. Instead, intentionally drop structured recipes (**CRAFT, PGTC, XML**) into the model to engineer repeatable, hyper-predictable results every single time.
